Suicidal Tendencies

And the Statistics

No, I am not referring to the band. Suicide has been an epidemic for the longest time and is continuously occuring. The rate of suicides is higher than homicides. Comparing the murder rate to suicidal rate, it is a ratio of 6 per 100,00 and 10.8 per 100,000. In the US, suicide is the 10th leading cause of death and happens every 13 minutes. In the world, every 40 seconds, someone takes their life. By the 24 hour mark, about an average 111 people on earth have committed suicide. It is definitely not something to be taken lightly. Suicidal thoughts occur more often than they should. For every 10 people who attempt to commit suicide, 1 person actually succeeds. Suicide mostly occurs due to mental or emotional disorder, which is 90% of the time. Within that, it is usually depression that causes this. Substance abuse also takes a crucial part within this issue. People who take their own life mostly occur within the white race, the next being American Indian. The highest amount of deaths from suicide (in 2016) is in Montana with a ratio of 24.3 per 100,00 suicides. New York had the least with a ratio of 8.4 per 100,000. In all honesty, I did not really know that there is money involved when someone takes their life. unless this does involve funeral arrangements and such. Within my research, I found that suicide costs society over $44.6 billion a year. This is a combination of medical and work loss costs. California ranks the 43rd state to commit suicide. A lot of people do not see how serious this issue is. People are taking their lives everyday for all these different reasons. Something that should not happen in the youth, middle-aged, nor elderly. The highest suicide rate is among adults between the age of 45 to 64, at a 19.6%. The second highest rate are those of 85 years or older with a percentage of 19.4. Young adults and adolescents (15 to 24) have a lower suicide rate with 12.5%. What is America doing to help exactly? Gregg Zoroya from USA Today points out "The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention promotes several "winnable" priorities, among them motor vehicle injuries and HIV. Suicide, though more costly in lives than either of those categories, is not on the list" in his article "40,000 Suicides Annually , Yet America Simply Shrugs". Usually when a person watches television or they are on their phone, computer, tablet, etc. and there are advertisements, a majority of the time it speaks about finding cures for cancer, feeding the needy, adopting pets, helping the disabled and even drug or alcohol abuse. Hardly is there ever an awareness towards suicide, though it happens everyday. Everyone knows about it and how much of an impact it takes on those close to the victim. It's not something anyone should accept if it can be prevented. There are people that can help with counseling and fighting these impulses that some cannot seem to overcome, especially not alone. What does it take for people to actually start finding options to help out the many people who feel like they are suffering from their own lives or from their mental illness? How about knowing that when someone dies by their own hands, part of their payments come out of their pockets? What about the pain and grief people face after hearing that their loved one has passed as fatally as they did? Or maybe they have to go through it themselves just to know the feeling and how serious it actually is? It seems to me through all my research, that despite how frequently someone takes thier own life, it is underestimated. Somehow, someone needs to create awareness of an epidemic that happens all over the globe, work to prevent the negative urges, and be able to allow everyone to live a full life as best as they can. My start is here. Where is yours?

Why Did I Choose This Issue?

I chose this issue because over the course of my high school years, I have came across more than one too many people who have thought, attempted or committed suicice. I never paid much attention to this problem until I found myself close to such a situation. I was able to help some people who felt this way and pulled them away from such a tragedy. Having to do something like that, especially on so many accounts, is still so unbelievable to me. To also think that things like this go on throughtout the whole world. Only the fortunate were able to be talked out of it, but some are not so lucky. Some may say they are lucky because the pain in their life was so dreadul and unbearable that taking their own life was them saving themselves. After coming into contact with people that found themselves having the weight of the world on their shoulders, it all became so opened to me of how big of a deal suicide and depression is. One friend of mine, for I know, is still struggling with it. Most have been able to overcome it, or they end up going back to that dark place, but are at least able to come out of it time and time again. Only one friend of mine had actually went through with his attempts, and even then, no one knew he felt the way he did. Some had at least recollection that he use to feel that way, me being one of them. Not many people notice how much of an impact suicide can be, not even I did until it hit me closer than it should hit anyone. So I want society to be aware of self-harm so we can help put a stop to it and allow everyone to live their life to its fullest potential.

My Story

On August 26, 2017, around 9:20 pm, I was in my bed, covered in my blankets ready to sleep. My mother was in the living room on the phone with my father (he was still at work, or at least on his way home from it). I hear a knock at the door and then the sound of my mom's footstpes heading towards the door. I assumed it was my dad, so I continued trying to sleep. Oddly, I didn't hear my dad's usual footsteps entering the door. My mom walks to my room and tells me my friend is at the door. My friend had apparently told her that it was an emergency. The first thing I thought was that she ran away from her house. She usually had family problems at that time. I quickly walk to the door and open it to find her and a young man behind her standing on my porch, and her mom stopped in front of my house. I crossed out the "runaway child" from my mind. I asked her what's wrong. Then the unforgettable and shcoking words came out of her quivering lips, "Bubbles is dead." I took a few seconds to process that. She called a good friend of ours, Nathan, Bubbles. It was his nickname. With disbelief, I looked at her and the stranger stadning near her. I thought to myself "How could this have happened? Did someone hurt him? Was it some sort of tragic accident?" She told me that he killed himself the night before. All of a sudden, my head was spinning. Within 2 minutes, everything seemed to become overwhelming. My mom came to the door asking what happened, in a stern tone. Turning to me for an answer, she saw the depression sinking in my face, and in a more comforting voice, asked me what was wrong. My friend answered for me, because I wasn;t able to bring myself to say those words. Eventually my dad came home, about 5 minutes after they arrived. My dad asked for my friend, the guy with her, and her mom to come inside. Inside my house, there was a more in-depth explanation of how it happened. The guy with my friend is friends of my friend as well as Nathan's. His name is Robbie. He told my parents and I that on the 25th, he was FaceTiming his girlfriend around 7 pm. Robbie's girlfriend mentioned that they were speaking, but Nathan was messing with a belt. Next thing she knew, he had turned the camera around, so she couldn't see anything. She thought it was a trick and hung up. An hour later after no response, she tried contacting him, but there was no answer. The next morning... Nathan's brother found him in the closet with a belt around his neck. He also had overdosed on ecstasy. No one saw it coming. No one. I had heard of his struggles before, but he told me that he had his reasons to fight in this life; me and my friend being one of them. It was so difficult to overcome. I use to tell him everything. We would even stay up to the small hours texting each other. We always talked about the deepest things about life. I even remember that I told him I hated the word hope, because it felt so useless and brought so much pain. So he told me, "Well then f*** hope and let's call it Nathan so I can make this world a better place for people in this world." Everyday I miss him. Sometimes other days more than others. But I always remember him. Even listening to a song that I can relate to will have me either in a blurry sight or just a complete saltwater discharge. I just wished that there was something I could have done to help him, so he can know that so many people that loved him would be so affected if he ever hurt himself. I just 'Nathan' that his pain is finally gone, and that he is looking after me and those who loved him, and that I will be able to see him again.

The Blackening

Natalie Jimenez

This is a poem that my friend's death inspired me to make a very short time after his passing in my English class.

The day was calm, the the night- a storm

The wind blew, the rain fell

It poured all night, until morning came

Yet the day was darker than last night

During that time, the world became forever altered


For there no longer was the sun

For there no longer were the stars

For there no longer was the moon

For there was darkness all around


The thunder pounded by the second without light

It began to rain hard

So hard that it drowned the world

An absence of hope for the light to shine

There seemed to be nothing left


Nothing left but the dark

Nothing left but the rain

Nothing left but the thunder

Everyting left but the light


The world remained in darkness

The world remained forever altered
