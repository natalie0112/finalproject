Suicidal Tendencies

And the Statistics

No, I am not referring to the band. Suicide has been an epidemic for the longest time and is continuously occuring. The rate of suicides is higher than homicides. Comparing the murder rate to suicidal rate, it is a ratio of 6 per 100,00 and 10.8 per 100,000. In the US, suicide is the 10th leading cause of death and happens every 13 minutes. In the world, every 40 seconds, someone takes their life. By the 24 hour mark, about an average 111 people on earth have committed suicide. It is definitely not something to be taken lightly. Suicidal thoughts occur more often than they should. For every 10 people who attempt to commit suicide, 1 person actually succeeds. Suicide mostly occurs due to mental or emotional disorder, which is 90% of the time. Within that, it is usually depression that causes this. Substance abuse also takes a crucial part within this issue. People who take their own life mostly occur within the white race, the next being American Indian. The highest amount of deaths from suicide (in 2016) is in Montana with a ratio of 24.3 per 100,00 suicides. New York had the least with a ratio of 8.4 per 100,000. In all honesty, I did not really know that there is money involved when someone takes their life. unless this does involve funeral arrangements and such. Within my research, I found that suicide costs society over $44.6 billion a year. This is a combination of medical and work loss costs. California ranks the 43rd state to commit suicide.
