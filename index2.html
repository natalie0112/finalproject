Suicidal Tendencies

And the Statistics

No, I am not referring to the band. Suicide has been an epidemic for the longest time and is continuously occuring. The rate of suicides is higher than homicides. Comparing the murder rate to suicidal rate, it is a ratio of 6 per 100,00 and 10.8 per 100,000. In the US, suicide is the 10th leading cause of death and happens every 13 minutes. In the world, every 40 seconds, someone takes their life. By the 24 hour mark, about an average 111 people on earth have committed suicide. It is definitely not something to be taken lightly. Suicidal thoughts occur more often than they should. For every 10 people who attempt to commit suicide, 1 person actually succeeds. Suicide mostly occurs due to mental or emotional disorder, which is 90% of the time. Within that, it is usually depression that causes this. Substance abuse also takes a crucial part within this issue. People who take their own life mostly occur within the white race, the next being American Indian. The highest amount of deaths from suicide (in 2016) is in Montana with a ratio of 24.3 per 100,00 suicides. New York had the least with a ratio of 8.4 per 100,000. In all honesty, I did not really know that there is money involved when someone takes their life. unless this does involve funeral arrangements and such. Within my research, I found that suicide costs society over $44.6 billion a year. This is a combination of medical and work loss costs. California ranks the 43rd state to commit suicide.

Why Did I Pick This Issue?

On August 26, 2017, around 9:20 pm, I was in my bed, covered in my blankets ready to sleep. My mother was in the living room on the phone with my father (he was still at work, or at least on his way home from it). I hear a knock at the door and then the sound of my mom's footstpes heading towards the door. I assumed it was my dad, so I continued trying to sleep. Oddly, I didn't hear my dad's usual footsteps entering the door. My mom walks to my room and tells me my friend is at the door. My friend had apparently told her that it was an emergency. The first thing I thought was that she ran away from her house. She usually had family problems at that time. I quickly walk to the door and open it to find her and a young man behind her standing on my porch, and her mom stopped in front of my house. I crossed out the "runaway child" from my mind. I asked her what's wrong. Then the unforgettable and shcoking words came out of her quivering lips, "Bubbles is dead." I took a few seconds to process that. She called a good friend of ours, Nathan, Bubbles. It was his nickname. With disbelief, I looked at her and the stranger stadning near her. I thought to myself "How could this have happened? Did someone hurt him? Was it some sort of tragic accident?" She told me that he killed himself the night before. All of a sudden, my head was spinning. Within 2 minutes, everything seemed to become overwhelming. My mom came to the door asking what happened, in a stern tone. Turning to me for an answer, she saw the depression sinking in my face, and in a more comforting voice, asked me what was wrong. My friend answered for me, because I wasn;t able to bring myself to say those words. Eventually my dad came home, about 5 minutes after they arrived. My dad asked for my friend, the guy with her, and her mom to come inside. Inside my house, there was a more in-depth explanation of how it happened. The guy with my friend is friends of my friend as well as Nathan's. His name is Robbie. He told my parents and I that on the 25th, he was FaceTiming his girlfriend around 7 pm. Robbie's girlfriend mentioned that they were speaking, but Nathan was messing with a belt. Next thing she knew, he had turned the camera around, so she couldn't see anything. She thought it was a trick and hung up. An hour later after no response, she tried contacting him, but there was no answer. The next morning... Nathan's brother found him in the closet with a belt around his neck. He also had overdosed on ecstasy. No one saw it coming. No one. I had heard of his struggles before, but he told me that he had his reasons to fight in this life; me and my friend being one of them. It was so difficult to overcome. I use to tell him everything. We would even stay up to the small hours texting each other. We always talked about the deepest things about life. I even remember that I told him I hated the word hope, because it felt so useless and brought so much pain. So he told me, "Well then **** hope and let's call it Nathan so I can make this world a better place for people in this world." Everyday I miss him. Sometimes other days more than others. But I always remember him. Even listening to a song that I can relate to will have me either in a blurry sight or just a complete saltwater discharge. I just wished that there was something I could have done to help him, so he can know that so many people that loved him would be so affected if he ever hurt himself. I just 'Nathan' that his pain is finally gone, and that he is looking after me and those who loved him, and that I will be able to see him again.
